Bayesian Data Assimilation for the SIR Model

Understanding the spread of infectious diseases requires reliable estimates of transmission and recovery parameters. In this project, we developed a Bayesian data assimilation framework for the classic SIR model. Our approach combined a physics-informed prior to encode known structural dynamics of the SIR equations, with a likelihood based on model residuals, penalizing deviation from the governing differential equations.

The SIR model (\(S =\) Susceptible, \(I =\) Infected, \(R =\) Recovered) is a set of differential equations given by \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{dS}{dt} &= -\beta S I \\ \frac{dI}{dt} &= \beta S I - \gamma I \\ \frac{dR}{dt} &= \gamma I \end{aligned} \] with \(\beta\) the Transmission Rate, \(\gamma\) the Recovery Rate and \(I_0\) the initial infected fraction of the population.

Our Bayesian framework allowed us to infer posterior distributions over the key parameters governing the epidemic, providing both point estimates and uncertainty bounds. The method can be used to integrate noisy or partial outbreak data with domain knowledge, yielding interpretable and trustworthy estimates in real time.
