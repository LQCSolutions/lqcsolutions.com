Bayesian Data Assimilation for the SIR Model

Understanding the spread of infectious diseases requires reliable estimates of transmission and recovery parameters. In this project, we developed a Bayesian data assimilation framework for the classic SIR (Susceptible-Infected-Recovered) model. Our approach combined a physics-informed prior to encode known structural dynamics of the SIR equations, with a likelihood based on model residuals, penalizing deviation from the governing differential equations.
